ABOUT THE PUBLIC ACCOUNTABILITY FOOTAGE ARCHIVE

ABOUT PAFA

The Public Accountability Footage Archive (PAFA) is an independent, non-profit archive publishing original law enforcement and public safety footage for public accountability, journalism, legal research, and historical documentation.

PAFA was established in 2022. All records in this archive are sourced, reviewed, and published exclusively by PAFA administration. PAFA does not accept public submissions. Footage is obtained through official agency releases, public records (FOIA) productions, and authorized distributions.

PAFA is not a government website. PAFA is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to any law enforcement agency, government department, or official body. The use of a governmental aesthetic reflects the archive's focus on public record and accountability materials.

OUR MISSION

PAFA exists to support public accountability, transparency, journalism, legal research, and historical documentation through the preservation of and access to links of real footage documenting interactions between the public and law enforcement, government surveillance systems, and other related video documentation.

We believe that:

  • Publicly available footage should be organized and accessible
  • Documented incidents serve the public interest when preserved
  • Journalists, researchers, attorneys, and citizens benefit from centralized footage archives
  • Transparency in policing and government activity is a public good
  • Publicly released or publicly visible footage is part of the historical record

HOW THE ARCHIVE WORKS

PAFA operates as a link archive. We do not host, store, upload, or re-distribute any video files. All footage remains hosted on its original platform (YouTube, Vimeo, Rumble, news sites, direct links, etc.). PAFA stores only the following metadata:

Field Description
TitleDescriptive title of the footage entry
CategoryType of footage (body cam, CCTV, police, etc.)
Video URLLink to the footage on its external hosting platform
Incident DateApproximate date the documented incident occurred
LocationGeneral geographic location of the incident
AgencyLaw enforcement agency or department (if applicable)
PlatformVideo hosting platform
DescriptionPAFA-authored narrative description of the footage and incident
Content WarningsApplicable content warnings identified during PAFA review
Record DateDate the record was published to the PAFA archive

All record metadata is maintained by PAFA administration. The archive is a static, client-side application. No user accounts or logins are required to browse.

ARCHIVE ACCESS & CONTENT POLICY

How records are added:

  • All footage records are sourced, reviewed, and published exclusively by PAFA administration
  • No public submissions are accepted under any circumstances
  • Footage is obtained from official law enforcement agency releases, FOIA/public records productions, and authorized distributions
  • Each record is reviewed by PAFA before publication
  • Some footage is pre-edited or segmented by PAFA to isolate the most relevant portions — all editing is documented on the record card
  • Some clips contain subtitles embedded by the releasing agency — these are original and unmodified by PAFA

What we do not publish:

  • Fabricated, staged, or fictional content
  • Content intended to harass or target private individuals
  • Content not sourced from an official or verifiable public release
  • Footage that has not been reviewed and approved by PAFA administration

PRIVACY POLICY

PAFA stores archive metadata in browser localStorage. No user account, login, email address, or personal identifying information is collected or required. No data is transmitted to any external server. PAFA does not use tracking cookies.

Submissions made through PAFA are stored only in the visitor's browser. Clearing browser data will clear all archive entries. PAFA does not use cookies for tracking purposes.

CONTACT / ADMINISTRATION

PAFA is maintained by its administrator. Updates, corrections, and content moderation are handled directly through the site's source code by the administrator. There is currently no public contact form. Administrative updates are applied directly via code.

LEGAL NOTICE

This archive exists under the principles of freedom of information, press freedom, and the public interest in documented accountability of government and law enforcement activity. All footage records in this archive are, to the best of PAFA's knowledge, publicly available at the time of publication.

If you are a copyright holder and believe a record includes a link to your content in an unlawful manner, please contact the platform hosting the video directly for takedown procedures. PAFA stores only the URL and will remove a specific entry from its database upon legitimate request by contacting the archive administrator.

■ BLURRED & REDACTED CONTENT POLICY

Many body camera, police, and officer-worn footage recordings contain blurred or obscured sections applied by the original releasing agency prior to publication.

This blurring is standard practice and is performed by law enforcement agencies, their legal departments, or court-authorized redaction processes for the following reasons:

  • Officer safety and identity protection during active operations
  • Privacy compliance for uninvolved third parties and bystanders
  • Ongoing investigation security requirements
  • Juvenile witness and victim protection requirements
  • Sensitive tactical information concealment

PAFA presents all footage strictly as received from its original source. We do not modify, alter, enhance, or further redact any footage content. During our standard internal review process, PAFA staff examine each record to verify authenticity and identify any sensitive elements. In some cases, reviewers may annotate or note redacted sections within the record. This is noted in the entry for transparency. The footage itself is the original file as released by the source.

When viewing footage from this archive, you may notice sections of video that are blurred, pixelated, or otherwise obscured. This is normal and originates from the source agency. PAFA cannot and will not provide unredacted versions of officially redacted footage.

💾 SERVER STORAGE & LINK EXPIRY POLICY

Due to the nature of body camera and police footage distribution, links to footage hosted on external servers are frequently removed or made unavailable after a period of time. Source platforms and agencies may delete, restrict, or move footage to manage storage capacity, comply with data retention regulations, or at the request of legal counsel.

PAFA strongly advises all visitors to:

  • Download or locally save any footage of interest immediately upon discovery
  • Check back to the archive regularly as new footage is added frequently
  • Subscribe to notifications to be alerted when new body cam footage is released to the archive
  • Bookmark specific entries for later reference, understanding links may expire

PAFA is not responsible for the continued availability of any externally hosted footage. Expired links will remain in the archive record as a historical reference.
