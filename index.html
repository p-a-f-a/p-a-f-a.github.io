WELCOME TO THE PUBLIC ACCOUNTABILITY FOOTAGE ARCHIVE

The Public Accountability Footage Archive (PAFA) is a community-maintained repository of publicly available links to real footage involving law enforcement, surveillance systems, body cameras, CCTV recordings, and other documentation of public safety and government activity.

This archive exists for public accountability, research, journalism, legal reference, and historical documentation. We believe in transparent access to public record footage.

ABOUT PAFA ORIGINAL FOOTAGE

What is PAFA? Details
Source All footage in this archive is sourced directly from official law enforcement agency releases, public records (FOIA) productions, and authorized distributions. No user-submitted content.
Original File PAFA publishes footage exactly as received from the source agency. We do not alter, modify, or re-edit any video file. This is the original footage.
Subtitles Some clips contain subtitles or captions embedded directly into the video. These are original agency-issued subtitles — added by the releasing agency, not by PAFA. We present footage as received.
Pre-Edited / Cut Some footage has been pre-edited or segmented by PAFA to isolate the most relevant and useful portions of a longer recording. Each clip is labelled accordingly. The original unedited source file is preserved.
Non-Profit PAFA is a fully non-profit public accountability project. No revenue is generated from this archive. Footage is distributed free of charge for public accountability, journalism, legal research, and academic use.
Blurred Content Some footage contains blurring or redactions applied by the original releasing agency for officer safety, privacy compliance, or operational security. PAFA does not apply or remove any blurring.

ARCHIVE POLICY

  • All records are sourced, reviewed, and published exclusively by PAFA administration — no public submissions accepted
  • All footage is obtained from official agency releases, FOIA productions, or authorized public distributions
  • Footage is presented in its original unaltered form as received from the source
  • Some clips have been pre-edited or segmented by PAFA to isolate relevant portions — all editing is documented on the record
  • Some footage contains subtitles embedded by the releasing agency — these are original and have not been added or modified by PAFA
  • This site does not host or store video files. Footage is linked from its original hosting platform (Vimeo, YouTube, etc.)
  • Body cam and police footage links are subject to server-side deletion — save footage immediately while accessible
  • Some footage may have portions blurred or obscured by the original releasing agency for officer safety or privacy compliance. PAFA presents footage exactly as received.
  • PAFA is a fully non-profit project. No revenue is generated from this archive.

🔔 SUBSCRIBE — NEW FOOTAGE NOTIFICATIONS

Due to the temporary availability of body cam footage links on source servers, we strongly recommend subscribing to notifications. When new body camera footage, police recordings, or other significant footage is added to the archive, subscribers receive an immediate alert.

Why subscribe? Body cam footage links are frequently removed from hosting servers shortly after being made available — sometimes within hours — to manage storage capacity and comply with data retention policies. Subscribers are notified first and have the best chance of accessing footage before links expire.
